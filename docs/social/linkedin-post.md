# LinkedIn Post — Ballot Badger / ElevenHacks

## Post text

This week I started building Ballot Badger for the #ElevenHacks hackathon, and I'm genuinely excited about where it's going.

The idea is simple: a voice-powered research assistant for Wisconsin's 2026 elections. You talk to it like you'd talk to a friend who happens to know everything about local politics. Ask "what's the deal with Tom Tiffany?" and it searches the web in real time, pulls voting records, campaign donors, fact-check ratings, and news coverage, then talks you through what it found while the findings appear on screen.

Here's how the pieces fit together. ElevenLabs' voice agent platform handles the conversation. When you ask about a candidate, the agent calls Firecrawl's search API to pull real-time data from Congress.gov, OpenSecrets, PolitiFact, the Wisconsin Examiner, and dozens of other sources. Claude synthesizes everything into structured findings. The voice agent narrates while UI components build on screen simultaneously. Votes, donor tables, fact-check badges, endorsement cards, all appearing as the agent mentions them.

Right now it covers 28 races across Wisconsin: the open governor's seat (9 Democrats, 2 Republicans), a Supreme Court election in 18 days, the attorney general race, three competitive U.S. House districts, four state senate battlegrounds, and three constitutional amendments on the ballot. Every candidate has a photo, party affiliation, and key facts. Every finding links back to its source.

What gets me thinking is what this could mean beyond one state. Midterm elections have an information problem. Voters care about local races but can't easily research who's running, who's funding them, and what they've actually voted for. Most people don't have time to read campaign finance filings or cross-reference PolitiFact. A voice agent that does this work for you, in plain English, with citations, could change how people prepare to vote.

Wisconsin is the test case because the stakes are real. First open governor's race since 2010. Democrats three seats from flipping the state senate for the first time since 2008. A Supreme Court race that determines the ideological balance for years. Three constitutional amendments. And all of it searchable by voice.

The hackathon runs through March 26. Prize pool is $19,480 across three places. I'll be posting the demo video across platforms when it's ready.

If you work in civic tech, election data, or AI tooling and want to talk about this, I'm all ears.
