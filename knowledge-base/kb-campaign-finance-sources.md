# Wisconsin Campaign Finance Sources & Key Data

## Where to Find Campaign Finance Data

### For State Candidates (Governor, AG, State Senate, Supreme Court)
- **Wisconsin Ethics Commission (CFIS)**: cfis.wi.gov — Official filings, individual donors $100+, PACs
- **Wisconsin Democracy Campaign**: wisdc.org — Searchable donor database, press releases with breakdowns
- **Transparency USA**: transparencyusa.org/wi — State-level finance summaries per candidate
- Do NOT use OpenSecrets for state-level candidates — it only covers federal races

### For Federal Candidates (U.S. House)
- **OpenSecrets.org** — Full donor/industry breakdowns for Congress
- **FEC.gov** — Official federal campaign finance filings
- **Congress.gov** — Voting records

### Key Finance Context
- Wisconsin's individual contribution limit for governor: $20,000 per election
- The 2026 governor's race is expected to be the most expensive in Wisconsin history
- More than half of Barnes' early money came from donors giving $10,000+
- Tiffany's fundraising total is approximately 2.5x the top Democrat (Crowley)

## When the Agent Discusses Campaign Finance
- Always cite the source (Wisconsin Ethics Commission, Wisconsin Democracy Campaign, etc.)
- Note the reporting period — data may not be current
- Compare fundraising totals to provide context
- Mention individual contribution limits ($20,000 for governor)
- Flag out-of-state money percentage when known
- Note PAC vs. individual contributions
