# Wisconsin 2026 Candidate Directory

## Governor — Democratic Primary (August 11, 2026)

### Mandela Barnes
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Former Lieutenant Governor (2019–2023)
- **Key Fact:** Lost 2022 U.S. Senate race to Ron Johnson by 1 point (~27,000 votes). Considered the Democratic frontrunner. Raised $555,000 in first 29 days of governor's race.
- **Background:** First Black person elected to statewide office in Wisconsin.

### Sara Rodriguez
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Lieutenant Governor (2023–present)
- **Key Fact:** First Latina elected to statewide office in Wisconsin. Former emergency room nurse. Raised $650,000 in first year.

### Kelda Roys
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** State Senator, 26th District (2021–present)
- **Key Fact:** Serves on Joint Finance Committee. Law degree. Former CEO of Open Homes non-profit. Previously served in State Assembly (2009–2013).

### Francesca Hong
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** State Representative, 76th District (2021–present)
- **Key Fact:** First Asian American elected to the Wisconsin Legislature. Member of the Democratic Socialist Caucus. Professional chef and restaurant owner.

### David Crowley
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Milwaukee County Executive (2020–present)
- **Key Fact:** First Black Milwaukee County executive. Won 2024 re-election 5-to-1. Previously served in State Assembly.

### Joel Brennan
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Former Secretary of the Department of Administration (2019–2022)
- **Key Fact:** Former president of Discovery World in Milwaukee. Served in the Evers cabinet. Business background.

### Missy Hughes
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Former CEO of the Wisconsin Economic Development Corporation (2019–2025)
- **Key Fact:** Economic development focus. Positioning as pragmatic/moderate candidate.

### Brett Hulsey
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Former State Representative, 78th District (2011–2015)
- **Key Fact:** Previously ran for governor in 2014 Democratic primary. Lost to Mary Burke.

### Zach Roper
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Student, Carthage College
- **Key Fact:** 22 years old. Youngest candidate in the race.

## Governor — Republican Primary (August 11, 2026)

### Tom Tiffany
- **Party:** Republican
- **Current Role:** U.S. Representative, WI-7 (2020–present)
- **Key Fact:** Trump-endorsed (January 2026). Member of House Freedom Caucus. 98% Heritage Foundation score. Chairs Federal Lands Subcommittee. Only major Republican candidate — considered the presumptive nominee. Leaving Congress to run for governor.

### Andy Manske
- **Party:** Republican
- **Current Role:** Medical service technician
- **Key Fact:** 26 years old. Unusual for a Republican — supports legal marijuana and abortion access.

## Wisconsin Supreme Court — April 7, 2026

This is a nonpartisan election. The primary was cancelled because only two candidates filed. This race determines whether the liberal majority on the court holds. Justice Rebecca Bradley (conservative) is not seeking re-election.

### Chris Taylor
- **Ideology:** Liberal/progressive
- **Current Role:** Appellate judge; former State Representative (2011–2020)
- **Key Fact:** Has 160+ judicial endorsements. Endorsed by U.S. Senator Tammy Baldwin, Wisconsin AFL-CIO, and United Steelworkers.

### Maria S. Lazar
- **Ideology:** Conservative
- **Current Role:** Court of Appeals judge (since 2022); former Waukesha County Circuit Court judge
- **Key Fact:** Previously worked as assistant attorney general under Republican AG J.B. Van Hollen. Not a registered party member but widely identified as the conservative candidate.

## Attorney General

### Josh Kaul (Incumbent)
- **Party:** Democrat
- **Current Role:** Wisconsin Attorney General (2019–present)
- **Key Fact:** Running for 3rd term. Key issues: PFAS contamination litigation, gun safety, consumer protection. Won 2022 by 0.6% against Eric Toney.

### Eric Toney
- **Party:** Republican
- **Current Role:** Fond du Lac County District Attorney
- **Key Fact:** Lost to Kaul in 2022 by 0.6% (approximately 20,000 votes). Running again. Campaigned on law enforcement and prosecutorial experience.

## U.S. House — Competitive Races

### WI-3 (Driftless Region / Eau Claire) — TOSS-UP
This is the most competitive House race in Wisconsin. DCCC top target.

**Incumbent:** Derrick Van Orden (R)
- Won 2024 with just 51.4% — narrowest margin in Wisconsin's House races
- Attended January 6, 2021 Stop the Steal rally (says he left before breach)
- Reported for yelling at a teenage library page over a Pride display
- Reported confrontations with White House staff and on the House floor

**Democratic Challengers:**
- **Rebecca Cooke** — Running for the second time. Lost by ~3 points in 2024. Outraised Van Orden in Q4 2025 ($1.1M vs $931K).
- **Brad Pfaff** — State Senator, 32nd District (2021–present). Was the 2022 Democratic nominee for WI-3.
- **Katrina Shankland** — Former State Representative, 71st District (2013–2025). Ran for this seat in 2024.

### WI-1 (Southeast Wisconsin) — LEAN R
**Incumbent:** Bryan Steil (R)
- Won 2024 with 54.0%. Has $4.2M cash on hand.
- DCCC flip target but considered a stretch.

### WI-7 (Northern Wisconsin) — OPEN SEAT, LIKELY R
Tom Tiffany is leaving to run for governor. Open seat.

**Republican candidates:** Mary Felzkowski (State Senate president), Duey Stroebel (former state senator), Calvin Callahan (state representative), Michael Alfonso, Jessi Ebben, Kevin Hermening, Paul Schecklman.

**Democratic candidate:** Katrina Clark.

## State Senate — Key Battlegrounds

Democrats need to flip just 3 seats to take majority for the first time since 2008. 17 seats are up (odd-numbered districts). Republicans hold 12 of the 17. This is the first election under court-ordered fair maps (replacing gerrymandered districts).

### SD-5 (Waukesha Suburbs) — Top Democratic pickup target
- Open seat. Suburbs have been shifting since 2016.
- Lean Democratic under new maps.

### SD-15 (Fox Valley) — Swing district
- One of the 3 districts that will decide Senate control.
- Competitive swing area.

---

## No U.S. Senate Race in 2026

There is NO U.S. Senate race in Wisconsin in 2026. Tammy Baldwin (D) won re-election in 2024. Ron Johnson (R) is up in 2028.
